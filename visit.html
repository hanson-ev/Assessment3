Why Visit Stonehaven

Whether you’re after a picturesque coastal or woodland trail that is suitable for the whole family, are a history buff searching for architecture and artifacts lost to time, or simply looking to grab an ice cream for a walk down the beach, there is plenty to do in Stonehaven.
Foodies will be spoiled for choice by the number of family-owned cafes, restaurants and more. For example, did you know that Stonehaven’s The Carron Fish Bar is the birthplace of a true Scottish classic – the deep fried Mars bar?

The weather in Stonehaven is at its best in the UK’s summer months – in particular from July until September, where the weather is at its warmest.
However, winter at Stonehaven is far from uneventful! The Hogmanay Fireball Ceremony brings in visitors from around the world every year, and is truly a sight to behold!

Getting to Stonehaven
By bus:
From Aberdeen, Stagecoach buses 7, X7, and 7B.
By train:
Regular hourly trains arrive to Stonehaven from both Dundee and Aberdeen.
By car:
Head south from Aberdeen via the A90 main road for roughly 14 miles
Essential Experiences
Image courtesy of Andrew McKie Photography
Surrounded by stunning landscapes, sweeping cliffs and incredible views of the ocean, Dunnottar Castle sits atop the rock-strewn headland. The former fortress is steeped in history, having seen its fair share of battles, royal affairs, and scandals. The site itself has a history that goes as far back as Pictish times, harbouring the oldest Pictish fort ever discovered!
The pride of Stonehaven, Dunnottar Castle is an essential visit for those fascinated by the past – or even for those who simply wish to take in the unbelievable views!

A must-see attraction for the summer! Stonehaven’s open air swimming pool, first opened in 1934, is one of only two pools of its kind in Scotland. Sporting an art décor style, this heated, Olympic-sized pool and lido sees a variety of events for the public, such as inflatable and chute experiences for the little ones, live music, and even moonlight disco swims for the adults. Or, of course, if you simply want to unwind and have a swim under natural sunlight, Stonehaven’s open air swimming pool is a great place to go.
The pool is open from June to September every year. Click here for more information.
Image courtesy of Keep Scotland Beautiful
Visitors looking for a leisurely beach walk surrounded by beautiful scenery and a plethora of family-owned beach cafes and ice cream shops to choose from have certainly come to the right place at Stonehaven beach!
The beach spans 1.1km in length, with additional flat pathways allowing for accessibility and plenty of benches, making it a suitable journey for anyone.
